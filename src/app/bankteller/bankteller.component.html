Bank Teller was a project I completed whilst learning about mutex locks and the pThreads class in C.
But what does this have to do with programming?

Well, picture this:

Imagine you have 4 bank tellers and a line of 100 customers.

•Each teller can serve 1 customer at a time.
•Each customer can choose to either Withdraw, Deposit or Inquire.
•Each action takes a different set time.

The problem?

•How do you most efficiently assign all 100 customers to the tellers, ensuring no teller is never not serving a customer?
•How do you ensure no customer is assigned to a teller whilst the teller is busy?
•What happens if 2 tellers finish with their customer at the same time?

The answer is with mutexes!

Designed to simulate multithread processing and shared resource usage in computer operating systems through the analogy of a bank teller scenario. The program uses simple and broadcast mutex locks from the pthreads library to achieve synchronization between threads and allow for multiple threads to access a shared resource safely.
